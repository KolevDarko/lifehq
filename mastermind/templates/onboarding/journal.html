Hey Rockstar, are you journaling right?

I used to hate journaling until I started doing it right.
Until I started writing about the things important to me, my goals.

Writing about your goals, how you are moving forward or even what is setting you back helps to keep them in the front of your mind and in your subconscious.

After few days your subconscious magically starts popping out new ideas and solutions in your head.

At LifeHQ I created 4 journal types, Year, Month, Week and Day, designed to provide different levels of perspective.

  • Year journal - Define the major goals for the year using the S.M.A.R.T. strategy. It makes them concrete, actionable and achievable.
  • Month and Week - These serve the same purpose to break down your active goals into manageable pieces. Personally I only use the Week because it is easier to predict what I can accomplish in 7 days instead of 30.
  • Day journal - (My favorite) This is the journal you use to get yourself going in the morning and to reflect on the day in the evening. Reflection is a powerful tool to notice patters of productive or unproductive behavior throughout your day. The daily journal will help you master self reflection.

All journals come with templates specifically designed for organization, productivity and overall awesomeness.
